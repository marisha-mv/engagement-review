Engagement review

What am I actually paying for?

I've read the agreement and the expectation-setting document closely. Here's how it reads to me — and two clean ways I'm open to moving forward.

01 — How the agreement reads

It's written like an editing contract, not an agency one

Nearly the entire document describes what we owe you. Every one of those commitments is concrete and enforceable. When I look for the equivalent on your side, I find one countable obligation: four edited videos a month. Everything else — "solid strategic direction," "responsibility for the outcome" — is language, not accountability. And the results are excused before we even begin: "not a guarantee," "1 in 10 videos," "152 videos to reach 10k."

Defined for us — the client
→Pay six months upfront
→Organise and run filming days
→Deliver a Brain Dump Loom per video
→Show up camera-ready, on script
→Provide footage by the Friday cutoff
→Hit 48-hour feedback windows
→Name one decision-maker
→Trust the process for 90 days
→Expect videos to miss
Defined for you — the agency
→Deliver four edited videos a month
—No agreed success metric
—No reporting accountability
—No defined strategy deliverable
—No consequence for under-delivery

One side carries every obligation. The other carries an edit queue.

02 — Where I stand

I'll own my side. I need to see yours.

I'm not avoiding my responsibilities. I'll own the filming cadence, the Brain Dumps, footage on time, and the feedback windows — fully and without excuses.

What I can't keep doing is paying agency rates for editing with no accountability attached to it. So here are two ways to fix that. Pick the one that fits how you actually want to operate.

03 — Two ways forward

Choose the model. Then we'll paper it properly.

Option A

Editing partner

You focus on output. We own the growth.

Operate as a production partner. We keep strategy, ideation and distribution in-house. You produce excellent videos, and we pay for exactly what you deliver.

What you deliver

  • A finished, optimised video per accepted delivery
  • Thumbnail that actually looks like the talent
  • Title, tags, end screen — and the CTA in the first two lines of the description

How we pay

£3,000 / video

Scales with output — four this month, we pay four; six, we pay six; two, we pay two. No retainer, no upfront. Billed per accepted delivery.

Option B

Growth agency

You own the channel — and the retainer is matched by real commitments.

Operate as a true agency. You own the channel's growth, not just the edit. The retainer stays — but it's tied to deliverables on your side, defined with the same precision as ours.

What you commit to

  • One north-star metric, locked at kickoff — subs, watch time, qualified traffic or leads. We pick it together.
  • A monthly performance review against it
  • Accountability on what you control: CTR, average view duration, packaging, and publishing consistency on the footage we supply
  • Active strategy: title & thumbnail testing, description and CTA optimisation, idea pipeline, format and trend research
  • A 48-hour escalation SLA — if our inputs fall short, you flag it and propose a fix. No silent under-delivery.

How we pay

Monthly retainer

Billed monthly, in arrears. No six-month prepay.

Either model works for me. What doesn't work is paying for a growth partnership and receiving an editing service with nothing on the line.
